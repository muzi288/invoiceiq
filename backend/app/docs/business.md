## The Problem

SMEs receive invoices constantly — from suppliers, service providers, and contractors. They arrive via WhatsApp images, email PDF attachments, physical paper, and scanned documents.

Without a system, the process looks like this:

An invoice arrives. A staff member opens it and manually types the data into Excel or accounting software — vendor name, invoice number, date, every line item, every amount. Someone else checks it was typed correctly. The owner or accountant approves payment. The invoice gets filed somewhere, maybe. Payment is made.

This process is slow — 5 to 15 minutes per invoice. It is error-prone — humans mistype amounts and transpose digits. It is untracked — there is no record of who processed what. It is unstructured — data lives in spreadsheets nobody can query. It is duplicate-prone — the same invoice gets paid twice because nobody checked.

A business receiving 50 invoices a month spends 4 to 12 hours on pure data entry. That is time the owner or bookkeeper could spend on actual business work.

## What InvoiceIQ Does

InvoiceIQ eliminates manual invoice data entry by combining AI extraction with human review and financial controls.

The flow:

A staff member uploads an invoice — a photo, PDF, or scan. Claude reads the document and extracts all structured data automatically: vendor name, invoice number, issue date, due date, every line item with description and quantity and unit price, subtotal, tax, total, and currency. The extraction takes 5 to 8 seconds.

The staff member sees the extracted data and reviews it. This takes 30 seconds instead of 15 minutes. They correct anything Claude got wrong and confirm. An accountant or owner then approves the invoice as a legitimate business expense. It enters the financial records as structured data. At month end, all approved invoices export as CSV for accounting software or the company accountant.

Time per invoice drops from 15 minutes to under 2 minutes. For 50 invoices a month that is more than 10 hours saved.

## Why AI and Not Traditional Extraction

Traditional PDF extraction works by reading text at fixed positions in structured digital files. It works perfectly when invoices are always generated by the same software in the same format. It fails completely on everything else.

Traditional extraction fails on:
- Photos of invoices taken on a phone
- Scanned paper documents where content is an image not text
- Invoices from hundreds of different suppliers with different layouts
- WhatsApp photos taken at an angle or in poor lighting
- Invoices where total is written as Amount Due or Grand Total or Balance Owing
- Handwritten elements mixed with printed text
- Invoices in different languages

Claude [or other equilvalent AI models] reads a document the way a human accountant would. It understands context, layout, and meaning rather than looking for text at fixed coordinates.

Traditional extraction says: find the word Total and extract the number after it. This fails if the supplier writes Amount Due instead.

Claude says: I can see this is an invoice. The final amount owed is R1,500.00 because it is the largest amount, preceded by subtotals, at the bottom of an itemised list, next to language indicating a payable balance. This works regardless of how the supplier formats their invoices.

Claude handles any layout from any supplier, photos and scans not just digital PDFs, handwritten elements, mixed languages, ambiguous formatting, and inferring missing fields from context.

Traditional extraction is pattern matching. Claude is comprehension.

## How a Business Uses InvoiceIQ

### Daily Operations

Staff members upload invoices as they arrive throughout the day. Each upload takes under a minute. Claude/ AI  extracts the data in the background while staff continue other work. When they return to review, the data is ready.

### The Review Queue

The accountant or designated approver works through the pending review queue. For each invoice they see the original document side by side with Claude's extraction. They verify the data is correct, make any corrections, and approve or reject.

Approval is not about Claude's accuracy. Approval is a business authorisation — a person with financial authority confirming this is a real, legitimate expense that should enter the company's records and result in payment.

### End of Month

The owner or accountant exports all approved invoices as a CSV filtered by date range. This file goes directly into Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, or to the company's external accountant. No manual compilation. No spreadsheet wrangling.

### Management View

The owner sees a dashboard showing total approved spend this month, spend by category, top vendors, invoices pending review, and upcoming payment due dates. Financial position is visible at a glance without opening a spreadsheet.

## What Approved Actually Means

This is the most important concept to understand about InvoiceIQ.

When an invoice is uploaded and Claude extracts the data, the status is pending review. This means the data has been extracted but no human has verified it yet.

When a staff member reviews and corrects the extraction, the data is confirmed accurate. But the invoice is still pending review — accuracy is not the same as authorisation.

When an owner or accountant approves the invoice, they are answering four questions:

Did we actually order this? Is the price what we agreed? Is this supplier legitimate? Have we already paid this?

Approval is a financial control. It separates the person who creates or uploads a document from the person who authorises it. This is a fundamental principle in financial management. Without it, a corrupt staff member could upload a fake invoice and approve their own payment to a fictitious supplier. With it, two different people must be involved in every payment — one who uploads, one who authorises.

Only approved invoices appear in exports and financial reports. Rejected invoices remain in the system for audit purposes but are excluded from all financial calculations.

## Target Market

### Primary: Zimbabwe SMEs

The Zimbabwean SME market is underserved by financial software. Most businesses use WhatsApp for everything — supplier communication, invoice receipt, payment confirmation. InvoiceIQ meets them where they are.

Key characteristics of this market:
- Invoices arrive primarily via WhatsApp images and email
- Multi-currency is the norm — USD, ZWL, ZAR, GBP all common
- Limited accounting staff — owner often doubles as bookkeeper
- ZIMRA compliance requirements create pressure for proper records
- Low tolerance for complex software — must be simple and fast

### Secondary: SMEs Globally

The invoice processing problem is universal. Any business receiving more than 20 invoices per month has this pain. InvoiceIQ's architecture supports any market with minimal configuration changes.

##  Business Features

### Vendor Intelligence
Every approved invoice builds a vendor profile — total spend, average invoice amount, payment history. Invoices that deviate significantly from a vendor's average are automatically flagged for closer review.

### Due Date Alerts
Approved invoices with approaching due dates trigger SMS or WhatsApp notifications via Africa's Talking. No more late payment penalties.

### Payment Status Tracking
Staff mark invoices as paid after payment with a bank reference number. Owner sees approved but unpaid, paid this month, and overdue at a glance.

### Recurring Invoice Detection
Claude flags invoices that match previous invoices from the same vendor. Once confirmed as recurring, the system expects them monthly and alerts if they do not arrive or if the amount changes.

### Accountant Access
External accountants receive a read-only access link each month end. They see approved invoices and can export data without needing a full system account.

### WhatsApp Upload
Staff send invoice photos directly to an InvoiceIQ WhatsApp number via Africa's Talking. The system processes them automatically and replies with extraction results. Zero behaviour change required from staff.
